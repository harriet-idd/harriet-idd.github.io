Program Description

Interaction Design and Development is a three-year program at the George Brown School of Design. Interaction Design is an interdisciplinary field that studies various forms of design including user experience, interface development, graphic design and coding fundamentals. Here at George Brown students learn a multitude of skills that will allow them to pursue careers in both front and back end development of digital devices and environments such as websites, mobile devices and wearables.

  • Year 1

    In the first year of the program, students are given the basic skills to succeed in the program. Students learn how to use Illustrator, Photoshop, InDesign, XD, Maya, Atom, Arduino, and Excel. They are taught skills to conceptualize products (wearables, apps, websites, and interactive devices) design interfaces, design logos and icons, create 2D and 3D product shots, create successful information architecture and code in HTML and CSS. In addition the students learn about design culture, studying the history of design and exploring more fields of design than just interaction. design

    Semester 1
    • 2D Visualization (INTR1001)
    • Information Architecture I (INTR1002)
    • Visual Design (INTR1003)
    • Technology Systems (INTR1004)
    • Technical Drawing I (INTR1005)
    Semester 2
    • 3D Visualization (INTR1011)
    • Information Architecture II (INTR1012)
    • Information Design (INTR1013)
    • Cognitive Dimensions (INTR1014)
    • Technical Drawing II (INTR1015)
    • Design Culture (GHUM1029)

  • Year 2

    In the second year of the program, students build on and further develop many of the skills they gained in the first year. They continue 3D modelling and learn how to animate in Maya. Students learn how to effectively communicate design, brand and market products, test for usability, understand semiotics, develop interfaces, understand social networks and create interactive immersive environments.

    Semester 3
    • 3D Visualization II (INTR2001)
    • Interface Development I (INTR2002)
    • Communicating Design (INTR2003)
    • Usability Testing (INTR2004)
    • Branding and Marketing Strategies (INTR2005)
    Semester 4
    • 3D Visualization III (INTR2011)
    • Interface Development II (INTR2012)
    • Spatial Design and Immersive Environments (INTR2013)
    • Semiotics (INTR2014)
    • Social Networks (INTR2015)

  • Year 3

    In the third year of the program, students begin to develop newer skills that have only been touched on previously. This includes creating interactive videos, developing devices, creating interactive systems, working with augmented reality, learning about ergonomics and human-computer interaction, and lastly they begin to work on and build their portfolios. After the sixth semester, there is an optional seventh semester that is a placement in the industry.

    Semester 5
    • Interactive Video (INTR3001)
    • Device Development I (INTR3002)
    • Ergonomics (INTR3004)
    • Portfolio I (INTR3006)
    • Interactive Systems Project I (INTR3008)
    Semester 6
    • Augmented Reality (INTR3011)
    • Device Development II (INTR3012)
    • Human-Computer Interaction (INTR3013)
    • Digital Publishing (INTR3014)
    • Portfolio II (INTR3016)
